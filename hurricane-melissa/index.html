The app

Melissa Relief Connector

A mobile-friendly application that helps people affected by Hurricane Melissa identify what support they need and connects them with appropriate assistance.

The app should help a user

  • Select whether they are requesting help or offering help.
  • Enter their general location.
  • Select the type of assistance needed or available.
  • Indicate the level of urgency.
  • Receive a recommended next step or suggested match.
  • See an emergency warning not to rely on the app for life-threatening situations.
  • Submit information for human verification.

Suggested app-building prompt

A starting prompt for your development platform. Adapt it to your team's decisions on the canvas.

Create a mobile-friendly prototype called "Melissa Relief Connector."
The application helps people affected by Hurricane Melissa request assistance and helps volunteers or organisations offer support.
Include:
- A home screen with "Request Help" and "Offer Help" options.
- A form for general location, assistance category, urgency, number of people and a short description.
- Assistance categories for food, water, shelter, medical support, clothing, transportation, electricity, clean-up and building materials.
- A results screen that recommends a next step.
- A priority flag for urgent cases.
- A confirmation reference number.
- A privacy notice.
- A warning that life-threatening emergencies must be directed to official emergency services.
- A message explaining that all matches require human verification.
- A clean, simple interface that can be used easily on a phone.
Do not display private contact information publicly. Do not describe unverified matches as confirmed assistance.
Two minutes

Presentation format

Each team has about two minutes and must demonstrate:

  • The user and the problem.
  • The information the app collects.
  • One working user journey.
  • The AI or decision logic.
  • One risk and its safeguard.
  • One improvement they would make with more time.
